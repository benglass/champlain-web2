Course Objectives

This course is designed to research, explore, and gain competency in the complex and fascinating world of Web Design & Development. Every web design project has an end product produced using the standard web technology stack (HTML, CSS and Javascript). Understanding the steps and details of the technical process of website production is essential to every professional designer. We will cover Idea and Concepting (through Sitemaps and Wireframes), Creative Production (Comps and Style Guides), Production (HTML, CSS, and Javascript), and Logistics (Hosting, FTP, the Wordpress CMS) of Web Design.

Student Outcomes

In addition to gaining a broad understanding of the complex processes of web design and production, proper planning, technical details, and the potential pitfalls in the process, the student will develop his/her ability to prepare graphics and files for maximum quality outcomes whilst minimizing the cost of production, and be able to:

  • Define the steps of professional web workflow for various devices, platforms and publishing techniques.
  • Review special design considerations when working with digital images, image editing and layout and how those design choices effect the web development process.
  • Navigate the hardware and software used in the web design process, including platform requirements and pre-flight procedures.
  • Execute several representitive design projects; both in the conceptualization process and in the technical execution according to conventional professional web techniques.
  • Apply knowledge of production techniques to create web ready artwork using HTML, CSS, Javascript, and jQuery

Guidelines for Evaluation of Student Work

Each student will a series of mini projects throughout the semester focusing on specific areas of the course material such as typography, layout, and interactivity. These mini projects will include homework as well as in-class studio time. The final project will begin at the mid-semester point. Evaluation will be accomplished by means of the instructor grading projects based on project descriptions provided and will include written or oral critical responses to the student's project work.

Course Policies

Absence/Tardiness

Absence is excused only for valid medical or personal reasons that have been properly documented and communicated to the instructor, either prior to, or a soon after the event(s) as is reasonably possible. Regardless of the reason, it is the student’s responsibility to obtain missed materials and information, to complete all assignments and/or exam(s), or obtain a valid extension. Late arrival to a class session is disruptive, both to the teacher and to the other students, and reflects an unprofessional attitude and may result in the student being considered to have missed the class. Please do not bring food into the classroom or computer labs; beverages are allowed but must be in a closable container. More than 2 unexcused absences or 2 consecutive unexcused absences will result in the student receiving 0 credit for their participation grade (10% of the final grade).

Conduct

Students are required to conduct themselves in the classroom, studios, and labs in a respectful and constructive manner, and consistent with the guidelines provided in The Rudder. Additionally, cell phones and pagers must be turned off, instant-messaging, personal entertainment headsets, and non-course-related web surfing is strictly prohibited during class sessions. Performance regarding any of these items may inform the grading process at the instructor’s discretion.

Assignment Submissions

All assignments and projects must be submitted through the correct assignment in the Canvas shell for the course. Assignments that consist of multiple files should be combined in a single downloadable zip archive file. Assignments and projects are accepted up to a week late for a full letter grade reduction (10%). Assignments not submitted within 1 week of the due date will not be eligible for credit. Students may request an extension of an assignment's deadline at any time before the assignment is due but not after and extensions will be granted at the instructor's discretion.

Non-Completion

An extension for assignment work will be granted only for extenuating circumstances such as illness or serious personal problems. An extension can be obtained by submitting to the instructor properly authorized written documentation (e.g. note signed by doctor, or parent/ guardian, etc.) A grade of “Incomplete” can be obtained ONLY upon the request by the student and with the instructor’s consent, and will be issued only for substantial and clearly documented causes. Please refer to The Rudder for guidelines concerning withdrawal from a course.

Academic Honesty

Please refer to the Academic Honesty policy as outlined in The Rudder.

Special Needs

If a student believes that s/he has a disability requiring accommodation(s) in a class, the procedure is to contact the Coordinator Support Services for Students with Disabilities prior to, or within one week of, the first class session, or as quickly as possible after a problem has arisen. After receiving an accommodation form, the student should contact the instructor so that adjustments can be implemented in a timely fashion. The Coordinator has specific guidelines on providing appropriate support services to students with various types of disabilities.
